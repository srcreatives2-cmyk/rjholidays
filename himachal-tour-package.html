Why This Package

Package Highlights

The best of Himachal Pradesh — from colonial hill stations to Himalayan adventure towns.

Shimla Toy Train

Ride the UNESCO World Heritage Kalka–Shimla narrow-gauge railway through 102 tunnels and 864 bridges across pine forests.

Rohtang Pass Snow

Cross the Rohtang Pass (3,978 m) for panoramic Himalayan views and snow play. Gateway to the high-altitude Lahaul & Spiti valleys.

Solang Valley Adventure

Zorbing, paragliding, skiing, and snow tubing in the popular Solang Valley just 14 km from Manali town.

Dharamshala & McLeod Ganj

Home of the Dalai Lama and Tibetan government-in-exile. Visit Namgyal Monastery, Tibetan Museum, and the Bhagsu Waterfall.

Colonial Dalhousie

Walk along Subhash Baoli, explore Khajjiar (India's mini Switzerland), and enjoy the serene colonial-era churches and bungalows.

Hadimba Temple, Manali

The four-storey pagoda-style temple dedicated to Goddess Hadimba set amidst ancient deodar cedar forest — a must-visit.

Day-by-Day Plan

Detailed Itinerary

Eight days covering four iconic Himachal destinations at a comfortable pace.

1
Arrival

Chandigarh / Dharamshala → Shimla

Pick-up from Chandigarh Railway Station or Dharamshala. Scenic drive to Shimla (2,205 m). Check into hotel. Evening walk on The Mall Road and Lakkar Bazaar. Dinner and overnight in Shimla.

Dinner
2
Shimla

Shimla Sightseeing & Toy Train Experience

Morning toy train ride on the Heritage Kalka–Shimla railway. Afternoon visit to Jakhu Temple (Hanuman statue), Christ Church, Viceregal Lodge, and Annandale ground. Shopping at Tibetan Market.

  • Jakhu Temple — 33m Hanuman statue, panoramic city views
  • Christ Church — neo-Gothic colonial landmark, 1857
  • Viceregal Lodge — now the Indian Institute of Advanced Study
BreakfastDinner
3
Shimla → Manali

Shimla to Manali via Kullu Valley

Drive 270 km along the Beas River through the beautiful Kullu Valley. Stop at Pandoh Dam and Kullu town. Arrive Manali by evening. Check in and stroll through Manali Mall Road.

BreakfastDinner
4
Manali Local

Rohtang Pass, Solang Valley & Hadimba Temple

Early morning drive to Rohtang Pass (3,978 m) for snow and views — permit required, arranged by us. Afternoon at Solang Valley for adventure activities. Evening at Hadimba Devi Temple and Vashisht hot springs.

  • Rohtang Pass (3,978 m) — snow, views, Lahaul entry
  • Solang Valley — zorbing, paragliding, snow tubing
  • Hadimba Temple — 1553 CE pagoda in deodar forest
BreakfastDinner
5
Manali → Dharamshala

Drive to Dharamshala via Mandi

Check out from Manali. Drive 250 km to Dharamshala via the historic Mandi town. Arrive by evening. McLeod Ganj orientation walk and dinner at a Tibetan restaurant.

BreakfastDinner
6
Dharamshala

McLeod Ganj – Namgyal Monastery, Bhagsu & Triund

Visit the Namgyal Monastery (Dalai Lama's personal monastery), Tibetan Museum, and Library of Tibetan Works. Short hike to Bhagsu Waterfall. Optional Triund Hill trek (6 km one-way) for panoramic Dhauladhar views.

  • Namgyal Monastery — Heart of Tibetan Buddhism in exile
  • Bhagsu Waterfall — 30-minute walk from McLeod Ganj
  • Triund Trek (optional) — 2,828 m, Dhauladhar panorama
BreakfastDinner
7
Dalhousie

Dharamshala → Dalhousie via Khajjiar

Drive to Dalhousie (2,036 m). En route, visit Khajjiar — called "Mini Switzerland of India", a flat green meadow with a small lake. Arrive Dalhousie for evening walk at Subhash Baoli and Gandhi Chowk.

  • Khajjiar Meadow — UNESCO-recognised mini Switzerland
  • Subhash Baoli — historic park and viewpoint
  • St. Francis Church (1894) — colonial Gothic architecture
BreakfastDinner
8
Departure

Dalhousie → Pathankot / Chandigarh Drop

After breakfast, drive to Pathankot (80 km) for rail connections or Chandigarh (290 km) for flights. Trip ends with memories of Himachal's magical mountains.

When to Go

Best Time to Visit Himachal Pradesh

❄️

Winter

Dec – Feb

Snowfall in Shimla & Manali. Rohtang closed but Solang accessible. Romantic for couples.

🌸

Spring

March – May

Rhododendrons bloom, roads open, perfect weather. Best for sightseeing.

☀️

Summer

June – August

Peak season. All sights open including Rohtang. 15–25°C. Most popular.

🍂

Autumn

Sep – Nov

Apple harvest season, clear mountain views, smaller crowds. Great for photography.
