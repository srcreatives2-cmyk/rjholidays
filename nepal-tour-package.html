Why This Package

Package Highlights

A spiritual, scenic, and wildlife-filled journey through Nepal — the land where the Himalayas meet ancient living culture.

Pashupatinath & Boudhanath

Seek blessings at Pashupatinath, one of the holiest Shiva temples in the world, and walk the great Boudhanath Stupa — among the largest Buddhist stupas on Earth.

Kathmandu Durbar Square

Wander through a UNESCO World Heritage Site of intricately carved palaces, courtyards and pagoda temples dating back to the Malla dynasty.

Phewa Lake, Pokhara

Glide across the serene Phewa Lake in a colourful wooden boat, with the Annapurna range reflected on the water — Pokhara's most iconic experience.

Sarangkot Sunrise

Wake before dawn for a panoramic sunrise over the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri ranges from Sarangkot viewpoint — a moment that stays with you forever.

Chitwan Jungle Safari

A UNESCO-listed national park home to the one-horned rhinoceros, Bengal tigers and gharial crocodiles. Explore by jeep, canoe and on a guided nature walk.

Nagarkot Himalayan Views

Catch a breathtaking sunrise over the Langtang and Everest ranges from this hilltop retreat, just an hour from Kathmandu.

Day-by-Day Plan

Detailed Itinerary

An expertly paced 8-day journey balancing temples, lakeside leisure, wildlife and Himalayan views.

1
Arrival

Kathmandu Arrival – Pashupatinath & Boudhanath Stupa

Arrive at Tribhuvan International Airport, Kathmandu. After check-in and a short rest, visit the sacred Pashupatinath Temple on the banks of the Bagmati River, one of the holiest Shiva shrines in the world. In the evening, walk around the towering Boudhanath Stupa, spinning prayer wheels alongside Tibetan monks as butter lamps flicker at dusk. Return to your hotel for dinner.

Dinner
2
Heritage

Kathmandu Durbar Square & Swayambhunath

A full day exploring the Kathmandu Valley's UNESCO World Heritage Sites. Morning visit to Kathmandu Durbar Square, with its carved wooden palaces, the Kumari Ghar (home of the Living Goddess) and ancient pagoda temples. Afternoon climb to Swayambhunath — the "Monkey Temple" — perched on a hilltop with sweeping views over the entire valley. Evening free to explore the cafés and shops of Thamel.

BreakfastDinner
3
Scenic Drive

Drive to Pokhara – Phewa Lake Boating

Scenic 6–7 hour drive from Kathmandu to Pokhara, following the Trishuli River through terraced hillsides and mountain villages. Check in to your lakeside hotel. As evening falls, take a relaxed boat ride on Phewa Lake, watching the Annapurna range turn gold and pink in the sunset, and visit the island Tal Barahi Temple at the lake's centre.

BreakfastDinner
4
Sunrise & Sightseeing

Sarangkot Sunrise, Davis Falls & World Peace Pagoda

Early pre-dawn drive up to Sarangkot for a spectacular sunrise over the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri massifs — one of the finest mountain views in the world. Return for breakfast, then visit the thundering Davis Falls and the mysterious Gupteshwor Cave. In the afternoon, take a short hike or cable car to the World Peace Pagoda for panoramic lake and mountain views. Evening at leisure on Pokhara's Lakeside strip.

BreakfastDinner
5
Jungle Transfer

Drive to Chitwan National Park – Tharu Cultural Evening

Morning drive (approx. 4–5 hours) from Pokhara to Chitwan, Nepal's premier wildlife destination and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Check in to your jungle resort. In the afternoon, take a guided village walk through a traditional Tharu community, learning about their unique culture, food and architecture. In the evening, enjoy a vibrant Tharu stick dance cultural performance.

BreakfastDinner
6
Wildlife

Chitwan – Jungle Jeep Safari & Canoe Ride

An action-packed day in Chitwan National Park. Morning jeep safari deep into the sal forest and grasslands in search of the one-horned rhinoceros, spotted deer, wild boar and — with luck — the elusive Bengal tiger. After lunch, a peaceful canoe ride along the Rapti River to spot gharial crocodiles and resident and migratory birds, followed by a visit to the elephant breeding centre. Overnight at the jungle resort.

BreakfastDinner
7
Hill Station

Drive to Nagarkot via Kathmandu – Himalayan Sunset

Drive back towards Kathmandu (approx. 5–6 hours) and continue up to the hilltop town of Nagarkot, perched at over 2,100 m. Check in to your hill resort and enjoy a spectacular sunset over the Himalayan range, including views towards Everest on clear days. Optional: book the Everest Mountain Flight from Kathmandu for the following morning (subject to availability).

BreakfastDinner
8
Departure

Nagarkot Sunrise – Kathmandu Departure

Early morning, witness a golden sunrise over the Himalayas from Nagarkot — one of the best vantage points near Kathmandu. After breakfast, drive back to Kathmandu (approx. 1.5 hours) with time for last-minute shopping in Thamel before transferring to Tribhuvan International Airport for your onward journey, carrying memories of temple bells, mountain sunrises and jungle trails.

Breakfast

When to Go

Best Time to Visit Nepal

Nepal is a year-round destination with each season offering a distinct and rewarding experience.

🌸

Spring

March – May

Rhododendrons bloom across the hills, pleasant temperatures and clear mountain views. Best overall.

🌿

Summer / Monsoon

June – August

Lush green valleys and waterfalls at their fullest, but hill roads can be affected by rain and landslides.

🍂

Autumn

Sep – November

Crystal-clear Himalayan views, festival season (Dashain & Tihar) and ideal trekking weather. Our favourite.

❄️

Winter

Dec – February

Crisp, clear mornings in the valleys; Nagarkot & Pokhara views remain excellent, though hill nights are cold.

Nepal Tour FAQs

October–December and March–May are the best times to visit Nepal, offering clear skies, pleasant temperatures and the finest mountain views from Pokhara and Nagarkot. Winter (December–February) is good for the lower valleys but can be cold in the hills. The monsoon (June–September) brings lush greenery but also landslides on hill roads, so travel can be less predictable. We operate year-round and tailor itineraries to every season.
Indian nationals do not need a visa to enter Nepal and can travel with a valid passport or an approved photo ID such as a voter ID card. Children under 18 must carry a birth certificate if they do not have a passport. We recommend carrying a passport wherever possible for smoother border formalities and hotel check-ins.
