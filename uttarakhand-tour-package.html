Why This Package

Package Highlights

A spiritual, adventurous, and nature-filled journey through Uttarakhand — India's crown of the Himalayas.

Rishikesh White-Water Rafting

Tackle the thrilling rapids of the Ganges — from Grade 1 beginners' stretch to the roaring Grade 4 Marine Drive run. India's rafting capital at its finest.

Haridwar Ganga Aarti

Witness the mesmerising Har-ki-Pauri Ganga Aarti at dusk — a thousand oil lamps on the sacred river, chanting echoing across the ghats.

Char Dham Pilgrimage

Seek blessings at Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath and Badrinath — the four sacred shrines that form Hinduism's most revered yatra circuit.

Jim Corbett Tiger Safari

India's oldest and most celebrated national park. Jeep safaris through sal forests in search of the Royal Bengal Tiger and over 600 bird species.

Nainital Lake Boating

A serene row-boat ride on Naini Lake framed by hills and pine forests — the quintessential hill-station escape of the Kumaon region.

Valley of Flowers Trek

A UNESCO World Heritage Site bursting with 300+ alpine wildflower species in bloom from July to September. A once-in-a-lifetime high-altitude garden.

Day-by-Day Plan

Detailed Itinerary

An expertly paced 8-day journey balancing pilgrimage, adventure, wildlife and leisure.

1
Arrival

Haridwar Arrival – Ganga Aarti at Har-ki-Pauri

Arrive at Haridwar railway station or Dehradun airport. Check in to your hotel. In the evening, walk to the iconic Har-ki-Pauri ghat for the spellbinding Ganga Aarti ceremony — a ritual of fire, flowers and devotion that has continued for centuries. Witness priests swinging lit lamps over the flowing river as bells ring across the ghats. Return for dinner and rest.

Dinner
2
Adventure

Rishikesh – White-Water Rafting & Beatles Ashram

Drive 30 minutes to Rishikesh — the yoga and adventure capital of India. Morning white-water rafting on the Ganga (16 km Marine Drive run, Grade 3–4). Post-rafting: explore the suspension bridges (Lakshman Jhula and Ram Jhula), visit the Beatles Ashram (Maharishi Mahesh Yogi's famous meditation centre), shop for Rudraksha malas and Ayurvedic oils in the market. Sunset yoga session on the riverbank (optional).

BreakfastDinner
3
Pilgrimage

Yamunotri & Gangotri Dham

Early morning departure toward the Char Dham. First, visit Yamunotri — the source of the River Yamuna. Trek 6 km (or take a palanquin) up through pine and rhododendron forests to the temple and the hot springs. Then proceed to Gangotri, the glacial origin of the Ganga. Visit the temple and experience the pure mountain air at 3,048 m. Overnight in a dham guesthouse.

BreakfastDinner
4
Sacred Heights

Kedarnath – Helicopter Transfer or Trek

Drive to Gaurikund and take the helicopter to Kedarnath (subject to weather) or trek 18 km up to one of the twelve Jyotirlingas at 3,583 m. The Kedarnath temple, set against the dramatic Kedarnath glacier peak, is one of the most awe-inspiring religious sites in India. Attend the evening Abhishek puja. Descend and drive toward Badrinath area overnight.

BreakfastDinner
5
Pilgrimage

Badrinath Dham & Mana Village

Morning darshan at Badrinath — the abode of Lord Vishnu and the last of the Char Dhams. Visit the Tapt Kund hot springs at the base. Drive 3 km to Mana Village — the last Indian village before the Tibet border, home to the legendary Vyas Gufa (cave). Return to the valley for an overnight stay en route to Corbett.

BreakfastDinner
6
Wildlife

Jim Corbett National Park – Morning & Evening Safari

Arrive at Jim Corbett — India's oldest national park, established in 1936 and named after the legendary hunter-conservationist. Morning jeep safari through the Bijrani or Dhikala zone, searching for tigers, leopards, elephants, sloth bears and gharials. Afternoon at leisure. Evening jeep safari as the forest turns golden. Overnight at a wildlife resort.

BreakfastDinner
7
Hill Station

Nainital – Lake Boating, Cable Car & Mall Road

Drive 2.5 hours from Corbett to Nainital — the jewel of Kumaon. Enjoy a boat ride on the emerald Naini Lake. Take the Snow View cable car for panoramic Himalayan vistas (on clear days, you can see Nanda Devi, Trishul and Panchachuli). Stroll the famous Mall Road lined with shops, cafés and colonial-era buildings. Visit Naina Devi Temple. Evening at leisure.

BreakfastDinner
8
Departure

Nainital – Departure with Himalayan Memories

After breakfast, check out. Transfer to Kathgodam railway station (35 km) or Pantnagar airport (70 km) for your onward journey. Our team bids you farewell with memories of river rapids, temple bells, tiger tracks and lake sunsets to last a lifetime.

When to Go

Best Time to Visit Uttarakhand

Uttarakhand is a year-round destination with each season offering a distinct and rewarding experience.

🌸

Spring

March – May

Char Dham opens, rhododendrons in full bloom, perfect weather for trekking. Best overall.

🌿

Summer

June – August

Hill stations are lush green. Valley of Flowers blooms in July–August. Monsoon in foothills.

🍂

Autumn

Sep – November

Crystal-clear Himalayan views, Corbett peak season, Char Dham closes — ideal timing. Our favourite.

❄️

Winter

Dec – February

Auli skiing, Nainital snowfall, Rishikesh yoga retreats. Char Dham closed; lower altitude visits possible.
