Why This Package

Package Highlights

Ladakh's signature experiences packed into 10 unforgettable days.

Pangong Tso Camping

Spend the night in a heated tent on the banks of the world-famous blue lake at 4,225 m — a surreal, starlit experience.

Nubra Valley Camel Safari

Ride double-humped Bactrian camels through the sand dunes of the Nubra Valley — the "cold desert" of India.

Khardung La Pass

Drive over the legendary Khardung La at 5,359 m — once ranked as the world's highest motorable road.

Ancient Monasteries

Thiksey, Hemis, Diskit, Alchi — centuries-old Buddhist monasteries perched dramatically on cliff faces.

Magnetic Hill

The optical illusion road near Leh where cars appear to roll uphill against gravity — a fascinating Ladakhi mystery.

Zanskar Gorge

The dramatic confluence of the Zanskar and Indus rivers — a must-photograph landscape of layered rock and turquoise water.

Day-by-Day Plan

Detailed Itinerary

Acclimatisation days built-in for a safe and enjoyable journey at altitude.

1
Arrival

Arrive Leh – Complete Rest & Acclimatisation

Arrive at Leh Kushok Bakula Airport (3,524 m). Transfer to hotel. Complete rest for the entire day — this is medically essential. No sightseeing on Day 1. Light dinner, early sleep.

Dinner
2
Leh Local

Leh City – Monasteries & Local Market

After acclimatisation, explore Leh city: Leh Palace, Shanti Stupa, Namgyal Tsemo Monastery, and the vibrant Leh Market. Short gentle walks only.

  • Leh Palace (17th-century nine-storey palace)
  • Shanti Stupa (sunset viewpoint)
  • Leh Market — Tibetan handicrafts, pashmina, prayer flags
BreakfastDinner
3
Monastery Circuit

Thiksey, Hemis & Shey Monastery

Drive through the Indus Valley past the Magnetic Hill phenomenon. Visit Thiksey Gompa (the "mini-Potala"), Hemis Monastery (Ladakh's largest), and Shey Palace ruins.

BreakfastDinner
4
Nubra Valley

Leh → Nubra via Khardung La (5,359 m)

Cross the world-famous Khardung La pass. Descend to Nubra Valley — check into camp. Evening camel safari on the Hunder sand dunes. Visit Diskit Monastery with its giant Maitreya Buddha.

BreakfastDinner
5
Nubra to Pangong

Nubra → Pangong Tso via Agham-Shyok Road

Drive the thrilling Agham-Shyok road through remote mountain terrain to Pangong Tso (4,225 m). Check into lakeside camp. Witness the lake change from blue to green to violet at sunset.

BreakfastDinner
6
Pangong Sunrise

Pangong Tso Sunrise & Return to Leh

Wake at 5 AM for the iconic Pangong sunrise — the sky turns pink and orange over the blue lake. Spend the morning at leisure, then drive back to Leh via Chang La pass (5,360 m).

BreakfastDinner
7
Zanskar

Zanskar Valley – Alchi & Sangam

Drive to the Zanskar Valley. Visit Alchi Monastery (11th-century murals), the dramatic Zanskar-Indus Sangam (confluence), and Nimmu Village. Incredible layered geological formations along the Indus.

BreakfastDinner
8
Tso Moriri (Optional)

Tso Moriri Lake – High-Altitude Wilderness

Optional excursion to the remote Tso Moriri lake (4,522 m) — a wildlife sanctuary with bar-headed geese and Tibetan wolves. Perfect for photographers and nature lovers.

BreakfastDinner
9
Leisure & Shopping

Free Day in Leh – Shopping & Rest

Rest and recover. Visit the Ecology Centre, local markets for Ladakhi crafts, Thangkas (Buddhist scroll paintings), and semi-precious stones. Farewell dinner with cultural performance.

BreakfastDinner
10
Departure

Leh Airport Drop-off

After breakfast, transfer to Leh Kushok Bakula Rimpochee Airport for your onward journey. Trip ends with memories of the world's most dramatic landscapes.

Breakfast

When to Go

Best Time to Visit Ladakh

❄️

Winter

Jan – March

Chadar Trek on frozen Zanskar River. Extreme cold (-20°C). For experienced adventurers only.

🌸

Late Spring

April – May

Roads opening, apricot blossoms. Manali–Leh Highway usually opens late May.

☀️

Summer

June – August

All passes open, perfect weather 20–25°C, all sights accessible. Best overall.

🍂

Autumn

Sep – October

Crowd-free, crystal-clear skies, excellent photography. Our favourite season.

Ladakh Tour FAQs
